# Score Dash Project Guidelines

## Game Goal
- Feel like a luck/competition game, not a lesson.
- Practice reading without exposing the learning goal.
- Preserve success, curiosity, and the urge to play one more round.
- Protect the child emotionally: failure is part of the game, not a flaw.

## Tone
- Short, sharp, arcade-like language.
- Do not use educational wording such as reading practice, comprehension, or accurate reading.
- Do not over-explain.
- Prefer game language: stage, route, bonus, gate, combo, clear, decode.

## Visual Direction
- Strong colors: blue, purple, turquoise, pink, gold.
- Soft 3D depth, glow, capsules, rings.
- No cubes, blocks, or block-jam look.
- Clean, large, readable mobile screens.
- Less text, stronger visual hierarchy.

## Game Structure
- 10 stages.
- 15 questions per stage.
- 150 questions per run.
- True/false only.
- The first real question must count as question 1.
- A stage intro is a visual transition only, not a question.

## Progression
- Always show clear progress: stage X/10 and Y/15.
- Every 15 questions ends with a clear stage transition.
- Stage end gives a points bonus.
- Game end includes finish screen, share, and play again.

## Question Screen
- Include only essentials: stage, progress, question, two answer buttons, short feedback.
- Do not show live billboard, side colored circles, "rank 8", large ring around question, or "catch the moment".
- Ranking/billboard belongs in stage transition, not inside the question screen.
- From stage 2 onward, add one extra short factual line above the true/false statement to increase reading volume.
- The extra line must be factual, game-neutral, and not called reading, practice, hint, or intro.
- Do not split the existing statement into two lines just to create length.
- Do not reuse true/false question statements as the extra factual line.
- If a stage has a dedicated two-line question set, use it as-is and do not add another context line.

## Stage Transition
- Must feel like a reward.
- Include Stage clear, points, stage bonus, position jump, continue to next stage.
- Social/ranking progress can appear here.

## Feedback
- Short and game-like, not school-like.
- Do not reveal the reading goal.
- Good examples: good, nice, good streak, next stage opened.
- Feedback should feel like game momentum, not correction.

## Logic
- Stage intro must not affect question count.
- Each real question is counted once.
- Stage transitions must be clear and not look like another question.
- Progress and scoring must remain consistent across the run.

## Sharing
- WhatsApp/share may appear at stage end and game end.
- Share text should be short, competitive, and invite others to compete.
- Sharing must not appear inside the question flow.
- Sharing is external and should feel like part of the reward moment, not mid-game noise.

## Core Rule
Every code/design decision must answer:
Does this increase pace, tension, emotional safety, or the desire for another round?
If not, remove it.
